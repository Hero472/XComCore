I don't intend to make this a _game_. I'm trying to create a complete tactical engine, similar to the one used in the XCOM games, so that anyone can plug it into Unity, Unreal Engine, or any other game engine or even their custom one.

Its important to have systems while programming so later our job becomes a lot easier this is thus the problem I'm trying to solve with this niche area in gaming and hopefully there will be someone who will put hours in this stuff to implement it on their own. Systems are what we have in this world, systems for everything, that are just a cluster of different processes that may be really specific but solve a bigger problem together such as a marketplace where the pay wall is one of the most important systems of the whole page. or the algorithm to recommend what to do which has a whole other system on how it works. All this and more is what creates the bigger system. Nevertheless is important to remember the _Divide and Conquer_ in programming because here is what I have applied it the most.

This chapter lays out the principles that will guide through this document in every data structure and algorithm and what boundaries we drew with the system. Understanding the basics of all is essential to understand the very subsystems that will be on the _Tactical Game Engine_
